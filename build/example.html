                      <h1>Diploma in Management — Treasury — Finance Concentration (30 credits)</h1>
           
          
          <div id="top-content">
                      </div>
            <!-- End top content block area -->
          <div id="content-area">
            <div id="node-40659" class=" clearfix">
  
  
  
  <div class="meta">
    <strong>Offered by:</strong> Career &amp; Professional Develop.    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    <strong>Degree:</strong> Diploma in Management      </div>
  <div class="content">
    <h3>Program Requirements</h3>
    
    <p>Currently under review. Admissions will not be accepted for the 2013-2014 academic year.<br />
If you are now working in this challenging area or plan to move in this direction, this specialization will provide you with an in-depth understanding of corporate finance and treasury management as well as such areas as investment analysis and international finance.</p>
<p>Note: Corequisite courses are not included in the total credit requirement for the program.</p>
          <h4>Corequisites</h4>
    
      
              <ul class="program-set">
                  <li class="program-course">
            <a href="/courses/cec2-532" class="program-course-title">
              CEC2 532 Business Economics (3 credits)              *            </a>
            <div class="program-course-description">
              <div class="program-course-description-inner">
                <div id="node-17011" class=" clearfix">
  
  
  
  <div class="meta">
    <p>Offered by: Career &amp; Professional Develop. (<a href="/faculties/continuing">School of Continuing Studies</a>)</p>
          <p>Administered by: <a href="/faculties/continuing">Centre for Continuing Education</a></p>
      </div>
  <div class="content">
    <h3>Overview</h3>
    <p>
      Economics (CCE)              : Introductory micro and macro economic theory and practice in profit and non-profit organizations from a managerial perspective. Topics include: demand and supply analysis, production costs, firms' pricing and output determination, aggregate demand and supply, national income, output and employment determination, inflation, interest rate, exchange rate determination, fiscal and monetary policies.          </p>
    <p class="catalog-terms">
      Terms:              Fall 2013, Winter 2014          </p>
    <p class="catalog-instructors">
      Instructors:                        Ken Matziorinis (Fall)                  Ken Matziorinis (Winter)                  </p>
    
      </div>
  </div> <!-- /.node -->
              </div>
            </div>
          </li>
                  <li class="program-course">
            <a href="/courses/cms2-500" class="program-course-title">
              CMS2 500 Mathematics for Management (3 credits)              *            </a>
            <div class="program-course-description">
              <div class="program-course-description-inner">
                <div id="node-18323" class=" clearfix">
  
  
  
  <div class="meta">
    <p>Offered by: Career &amp; Professional Develop. (<a href="/faculties/continuing">School of Continuing Studies</a>)</p>
          <p>Administered by: <a href="/faculties/continuing">Centre for Continuing Education</a></p>
      </div>
  <div class="content">
    <h3>Overview</h3>
    <p>
      Management Science (CCE)              : Basic mathematics needed for business applications, including graphs of functions, series summation, mathematics of finance, annuity, discounted cash flow, internal rate of return, permutations, combinations, maxima and minima of functions with business applications in optimization, introductory statistics and probability          </p>
    <p class="catalog-terms">
      Terms:              Fall 2013, Winter 2014          </p>
    <p class="catalog-instructors">
      Instructors:                        Galina Golovina, Maria Eduarda Nunes (Fall)                  Galina Golovina, Jim Norman Graham (Winter)                  </p>
    
          <ul class="catalog-notes">
              <li>Prerequisite: CMSC 000</li>
              <li>Note: "Mathematics for Management" must be completed in the first semester of the Diploma in Management program.</li>
            </ul>
      </div>
  </div> <!-- /.node -->
              </div>
            </div>
          </li>
                </ul>
      
              <p>* or the Exemption by Examination Test</p>
              * or the Exemption by Examination Test                <h4>Required Courses (27 credits)</h4>
      
                <h4>Required Courses (27 credits)</h4>
    
      
              <ul class="program-set">
                  <li class="program-course">
            <a href="/courses/cacc-520" class="program-course-title">
              CACC 520 Accounting for Management (3 credits)              <!---->            </a>
            <div class="program-course-description">
              <div class="program-course-description-inner">
                <div id="node-16657" class=" clearfix">
  
  
  
  <div class="meta">
    <p>Offered by: Career &amp; Professional Develop. (<a href="/faculties/continuing">School of Continuing Studies</a>)</p>
          <p>Administered by: <a href="/faculties/continuing">Centre for Continuing Education</a></p>
      </div>
  <div class="content">
    <h3>Overview</h3>
    <p>
      Accountancy              : This course covers financial and managerial accounting. The course provides an understanding of the various financial statements as well as cost behaviour, cost/volume/profit relationships, budgets, responsibility accounting and relevant costing.          </p>
    <p class="catalog-terms">
      Terms:              Fall 2013, Winter 2014          </p>
    <p class="catalog-instructors">
      Instructors:                        Thierry Cohen, Deirdre Lapointe (Fall)                  Deirdre Lapointe, Thierry Cohen (Winter)                  </p>
    
          <ul class="catalog-notes">
              <li>Corequisite: CMS2 500</li>
            </ul>
      </div>
  </div> <!-- /.node -->
              </div>
            </div>
          </li>
                  <li class="program-course">
            <a href="/courses/cfin-512" class="program-course-title">
              CFIN 512 Corporate Finance (3 credits)              <!---->            </a>
            <div class="program-course-description">
              <div class="program-course-description-inner">
                <div id="node-17233" class=" clearfix">
  
  
  
  <div class="meta">
    <p>Offered by: Career &amp; Professional Develop. (<a href="/faculties/continuing">School of Continuing Studies</a>)</p>
          <p>Administered by: <a href="/faculties/continuing">Centre for Continuing Education</a></p>
      </div>
  <div class="content">
    <h3>Overview</h3>
    <p>
      Finance (CCE)              : Fundamental finance theory as applied to the firm's short and long-term financing and investment decisions and the sources of funds available to it (stocks, bonds, derivatives). Exposure to critical concepts of "firm value maximization" emphasizing capital budgeting, cost of capital, capital structure, derivatives, dividend policy, risk and return.          </p>
    <p class="catalog-terms">
      Terms:              Fall 2013, Winter 2014          </p>
    <p class="catalog-instructors">
      Instructors:                        Raad Jassim, Julie Slater-Santini (Fall)                  Greg Gregoriou, Julie Slater-Santini (Winter)                  </p>
    
          <ul class="catalog-notes">
              <li>Prerequisites: CACC 520 or CACC 521 and CMS2 521</li>
            </ul>
      </div>
  </div> <!-- /.node -->
              </div>
            </div>
          </li>
                  <li class="program-course">
            <a href="/courses/cfin-522" class="program-course-title">
              CFIN 522 Applied Topics: Corporate Finance (3 credits)              <!---->            </a>
            <div class="program-course-description">
              <div class="program-course-description-inner">
                <div id="node-17237" class=" clearfix">
  
  
  
  <div class="meta">
    <p>Offered by: Career &amp; Professional Develop. (<a href="/faculties/continuing">School of Continuing Studies</a>)</p>
          <p>Administered by: <a href="/faculties/continuing">Centre for Continuing Education</a></p>
      </div>
  <div class="content">
    <h3>Overview</h3>
    <p>
      Finance (CCE)              : Concepts and techniques developed in earlier courses are extended and/or applied to problems faced by managers in Corporate Finance. Such problems include: working capital management, capital budgeting, capital structure, dividend policy, cost of capital and mergers and acquisitions. Stresses the application of theory and techniques; and makes extensive use of case studies.          </p>
    <p class="catalog-terms">
